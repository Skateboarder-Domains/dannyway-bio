Legendary Footage That Defined Skateboarding

Iconic Video Parts

Danny Way's video parts have become legendary in skateboarding history, showcasing his fearless approach, technical innovation, and groundbreaking tricks that pushed the sport forward.

Plan B - Questionable (1992)

One of the most influential skateboard videos ever made, "Questionable" featured Danny's groundbreaking part that helped define street skateboarding in the early '90s. His innovative tricks and fearless approach set new standards for what was possible.

Directed by Mike Ternasky, this video showcased the entire Plan B team at their peak, with Danny's section standing out for its technical prowess and creativity.

Plan B - Virtual Reality (1993)

"Virtual Reality" continued Plan B's dominance in skateboard video production. Danny's part featured even more progressive tricks and cemented his status as one of skateboarding's most innovative riders.

This video is considered one of the greatest skateboard videos of all time, with Danny's section showcasing tricks that many skateboarders still find challenging today.

DC Video (2003)

As co-founder of DC Shoes, Danny's parts in DC videos showcased his evolution as a skater and his work on larger ramps and more progressive terrain. These parts bridged the gap between his street skating roots and his Mega Ramp innovations.
