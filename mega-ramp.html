The Mega Ramp

Redefining What's Possible

Danny Way's Revolutionary Innovation in Skateboarding

Revolutionary Innovation

The Mega Ramp represents one of the most significant innovations in skateboarding history. Pioneered by Danny Way, this massive structure fundamentally changed what was possible on a skateboard, enabling unprecedented heights, distances, and progressive tricks that were previously unimaginable.

Unlike traditional skateboard ramps, the Mega Ramp is a colossal structure featuring a steep roll-in, a massive gap, and a towering quarterpipe. This design allows skateboarders to achieve speeds of 40+ mph and launch themselves to heights exceeding 20 feet, opening up entirely new dimensions of progression in the sport.

The Development Story

The Mega Ramp didn't emerge overnight‚Äîit was the result of Danny's continuous quest to push skateboarding's boundaries. Throughout the 1990s, Danny progressively built larger and larger ramps, each one designed to enable tricks and heights that were previously impossible.

Danny's vision was clear: create a structure that would allow skateboarders to achieve the kind of amplitude and airtime that would enable entirely new categories of tricks. This required not just physical construction, but also engineering expertise to ensure safety while maintaining the massive scale required for progression.

Working with engineers and builders, Danny refined the Mega Ramp design through countless iterations. Each version incorporated lessons learned from previous attempts, gradually perfecting the angles, transitions, and structural integrity needed to make the vision a reality.
